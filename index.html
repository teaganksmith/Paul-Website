Paul E. Patton

Ph.D. Candidate · History & Philosophy of Science

I am a doctoral candidate at the Institute for the History and Philosophy of Science at the University of Toronto. My research moves between the philosophy of perception, the social epistemology of science, and the formal study of scientific change — an emerging field known as scientonomy.

Before turning to history and philosophy of science, I trained as a neurobiologist, with a Ph.D. from the University of Chicago and research positions at Illinois, Arizona, and Bowling Green State University. That background continues to inform how I think about perception, cognition, and the practice of empirical inquiry.

Current Work

My present research develops a perspectival perceptual realism: an account of perception that treats sensory qualities such as colour as real features of the world disclosed from a point of view, rather than as projections of the mind onto a colourless physical reality. The project sits between phenomenology, cognitive science, and the metaphysics of perception.

I am also a co-editor in chief of the Encyclopedia of Scientonomy, and co-author of more than three hundred of its articles. With Hakob Barseghyan and Jamie Shaw, I am co-editing a forthcoming volume, Visualizing Worldviews: A Diagrammatic Notation for Belief Systems.

Writing for the Public

Alongside my academic work, I have written 21 feature magazine articles — chiefly for Universe Today, with earlier pieces in Scientific American Mind and Astronomy — on astrobiology, the search for extraterrestrial intelligence, and the philosophy of life and mind. Since 2014 I have authored more than five hundred radio scripts for Indiana Public Media's A Moment of Science.

A complete list of articles, peer-reviewed papers, abstracts, and talks is on the publications page.
